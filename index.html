Youtube in myth of Political Neutrality

Is youtube strictly politically unbiased or does it have its own preferences?

Introduction

American electoral politics have been dominated by two major political parties: the Democratic Party and the Republican Party. While democrats are liberal and left-leaning, republicans adopted a conservative right-leaning ideology. Each year, the world watches how these two powerful parties dispute power and pick sides. In this context, does Youtube have a political orientation? Is youtube strictly politically unbiased or does it have its own preferences?

In this article, we explore the prevalence of videos on YouTube that align with different political spectrums. We analyze the content and messages of these videos, as well as their popularity during the major events on the US politics scene. Through this analysis, we aim to shed light on the role that YouTube plays in shaping political views and the potential for bias on the platform.

Why is it important?

YouTube is the world's largest video hosting service. In the recent years, Youtube audience grew more than 10 times: from 200 millions in 2010 to 2.5 billions in 2021. Such platform with a large user base has the potential to shape public discourse and influence the way people think about certain issues. If it is perceived as biased, it may only amplify certain viewpoints and silence others, leading to a distorted public discourse. Political bias can contribute to the polarization of society, as people are exposed to more one-sided views and may be less likely to engage with viewpoints that differ from their own.

Overall, it is important for platforms like YouTube to remain neutral and unbiased in order to maintain the trust and support of their users and avoid legal and reputational risks.

YouTube is a popular and widely used platform with a significant user base. In 2021, YouTube had over 2 billion logged-in users per month[1], making it one of the most visited websites in the world. In addition, the platform had over 1 billion hours of content watched per day[2] and was available in over 100 countries and in 80 languages. These statistics suggest that YouTube was a highly popular and influential platform at the time of my knowledge cutoff. It is possible that YouTube's popularity and user base have continued to grow since then.

Research questions

Our research aims to find the answer to a series of question that would help us understand the political polarization of Youtube:

  • Is YouTube strictly politically unbiased or does it have its own preferences?
  • Do democrats and republicans live in partisan bubbles or is there any conversation between the two sides?
  • Is it a bad thing for a channel to change the direction of the videos that they are producing? Does this affects the overall engagement?
  • Do we see an engagement in comments from both political spectrums, or people prefer not to engage in "comment battles" with each other?
